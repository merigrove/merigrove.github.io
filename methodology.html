Stage 01 · The Audit
Stage 01

Diagnosing the business before prescribing the solution.

A structured diagnostic across four core domains. The output is a ranked priority list — not a broad recommendation deck — that forms the precise foundation of the playbook.

Domain 01

Sales & Revenue

Pipeline health, conversion rates at each funnel stage, sales team performance, CRM hygiene, and the quality of the sales management layer.

Domain 02

Marketing & Branding

Lead generation volume and quality, channel performance, brand consistency, offer positioning, and content-to-pipeline attribution.

Domain 03

Finance & Operations

Unit economics, margin profile, cash flow patterns, financial reporting quality, operational workflows, and technology cost structure.

Domain 04

Management Layer

Decision-making velocity, team structure, accountability systems, meeting cadence, KPI visibility, and whether leadership is a bottleneck or a multiplier.

01

Discovery Call

Structured conversation with the founder covering all four domains. Surfaces the acute pain, the underlying cause, and the founder's theory of what is wrong.

02

Discovery Questionnaire

Written submission capturing quantitative data — revenue, conversion rates, team size, CAC, LTV, margin — plus qualitative context on history and blockers.

Output: ranked priority list across all four domains → forms the basis of the initial three-to-six month playbook

Stage 02 · Engagement Model
Stage 02

Two ways to work with Merigrove.

The right model is determined during scoping, based on the client's needs, internal capacity, and the nature of the work to be executed. Some engagements blend both models across different workstreams.

Done For You — DFY

Full operational embedding.

A Merigrove specialist is onboarded into your business as a fractional contractor with access to your systems, tools, and day-to-day operations. We execute directly — not from the outside.

  • Onboarded into client systems with full operational access
  • Attends relevant meetings and works within the client's environment
  • Takes direct ownership of defined workstreams day-to-day
  • Suited to clients who need hands-on execution capacity
Done With You — DWY

Advisory and consulting.

A Merigrove specialist works alongside your team rather than inside it — providing direction, structured frameworks, and accountability. The client's team handles hands-on execution.

  • Weekly calls with the specialist and corresponding client team
  • Delivery of memos, frameworks, and structured recommendations
  • Client team owns all implementation
  • Suited to clients with internal capacity who need direction and challenge

Part 01 · Revenue Optimisation
Part 01

Revenue Optimisation.

Growth, scalability, and predictability. A business with all three grows quickly, absorbs scale without breaking, and stops depending on exceptional months to hit its targets.

1.1

Revenue Growth

Improve pipeline output and close rates using existing resources. The fastest return available — current team and infrastructure are underperforming, and systematic improvement compounds immediately. Covers the full funnel from lead generation to closed revenue.

1.2

Revenue Scalability

Put systems in place that allow revenue to grow without proportionally increasing management effort or headcount. Move from a business that scales people to one that scales systems. Onboarding, CRM architecture, automated workflows, management cadence, and recruitment.

Sales Operations Sales Management Marketing & Branding Management
1.3

Revenue Predictability

Give founders the visibility to forecast reliably, reduce dependence on exceptional months, and protect the revenue engine they have built. Unit economics, pipeline reverse-engineering, corporate brand independence, and long-term client value engineering.

Part 02 · Profit Maximisation
Part 02

Profit Maximisation.

Operational and financial efficiency. The most common failure mode in scaling businesses is costs growing faster than revenue. This part addresses the root causes of margin erosion before it compounds.

2.1

Operational Efficiency

Identify and remove friction in the management and operational layer that is costing time, money, or decision quality. Install the management operating system — productivity systems, meeting cadence, KPIs and reviews for the leadership team — and automate workflows across the wider business.

Management Operations
2.2

Financial Efficiency

Ensure the financial structure is transparent, well-managed, and optimised for profitability — not just top-line growth. Monthly management accounts, accrual vs. cash advisory, business protection metrics (current ratio, gross margin by line), pricing analysis, and a full people and technology cost review.
