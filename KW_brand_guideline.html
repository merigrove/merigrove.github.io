01 — Ethos

The Foundation

Brutal honesty. Earned authority. Human enough to trust.

Most personal brands are built on perception. Kacper's is built on proof.

He does not tell people what they want to hear. He tells them what they need to hear, and then shows them exactly what to do about it. The ethos of this brand is straightforward: real results come from real structure. Not from motivation, not from hustle culture, and not from louder leadership.

This brand stands for the belief that a good system protects people. That discipline is not punishment; it is the architecture of freedom. That the best leaders do not shout louder. They build environments where people can perform without fear, without burnout, and without constant oversight.

Everything created under this brand — every post, every video, every keynote, every offer — must pass a single test: Is this true? Is it useful? Is it earned?

02 — Mission

The Work

To fix broken sales systems, build disciplined teams, and create environments where people perform — without fear, burnout, or babysitting.

Kacper exists in the market to solve one specific problem: the gap between what a sales team could produce and what it actually produces. That gap is not caused by lazy reps. It is caused by structural failure at the leadership level.

His mission is to close that gap. Practically. Permanently.

Not through motivational content. Through systems, standards, and accountability that outlast his involvement.

03 — Vision

The Long Game

A world where leaders stop blaming their people and start building the structures that make their people great.

The long-term vision is a generation of sales leaders and founders who understand that performance is a leadership responsibility, not a rep problem. Leaders who can step away from their business for a week, a month, or longer, and return to something that still runs.

Kacper's personal north star is to be the person he needed when he was the broke, invisible rep that nobody believed in, and to make sure others never have to figure it out alone.

04 — Values

Non-Negotiables

Discipline over talent

Motivation fades. Talent is inconsistent. Discipline compounds. Everything Kacper teaches, sells, and lives is rooted in the belief that showing up with standards, every day regardless of how you feel, is what separates the people who build something real from the people who stay stuck.

Accountability without blame

Accountability is not about punishing people. It is about creating clarity so that everyone knows what is expected, what success looks like, and where they stand. Blame is lazy. Real accountability is structural.

Standards as protection

High standards do not crush people. Low standards do. When expectations are clear and consistent, people know where they stand, they can grow, and they feel safe. This brand explicitly rejects the idea that being demanding and being supportive are opposites.

Integrity at all costs

His word is currency. If he says he will deliver, he delivers — regardless of inconvenience. He will not lower expectations to make people comfortable, and he will not tell people what they want to hear if it is not true.

Congruency

What he teaches, he lives. The gym. The standards. The discipline. The honesty. This brand is not a persona. It is a direct expression of how he operates every day.

Value over capital

He walked away from a role that was only about extracting revenue. The intention behind everything he builds is to leave people better than he found them — not just stronger in results, but stronger in capability.

The Beginning

Pizza delivery. No path. No plan.

Kacper Wiloch grew up with no obvious path forward. No connections. No university plan. No safety net. Where he came from, you either accepted the ceiling above you or you fought your way through it. He was a pizza delivery boy. Overweight. Earning €500 a month. Invisible, even to himself. There was no mentor. No system. Nobody coming to save him.

2019 — The Turning Point

Sold everything. Went into debt.

He made a decision that most people in his position would not. He sold everything he had and went into debt to invest in high-ticket sales training. The first six months produced almost nothing. Friends doubted him. Family did not understand. He barely believed in himself. Every day ended with the same question: quit and stay stuck forever, or level up into someone people could not ignore?

The Build

Discipline as oxygen.

He treated discipline like oxygen. Every spare pound went into learning. He studied his failures obsessively. He ran. He trained in MMA. He showed up when he hated it. Slowly, the numbers changed: £300 a month in commissions to personally closing $5M+. Rep to Manager to Sales Director. A sales organisation scaled from $20M to $39M in 12 months. Teams responsible for $70M+ in cash collected.

Today

The agency. The mission.

He walked away from a role that was only capturing capital. He built an agency that generates in days what used to take a year — not because he wanted the money, but because he wanted to create something that lasts. His authority is not a certificate. Not a pedigree. It is the lived experience of having sat in every seat: rep, manager, owner.

"I was the rep that got failed by management. That is why I can tell you what your team is thinking, even when they will not say it out loud."

07 — Voice and Tone

How It Sounds

Direct. Earned. Uncompromising, but never cold.

1. Say the thing

Kacper does not soften hard truths to protect feelings. He names the problem directly and without apology. His audience respects him because he respects them enough to be honest.

"Throwing reps into Slack and expecting numbers by Friday is not leadership. It is abandonment with blame attached."
"There are some areas where onboarding could be improved."
2. Earn every claim

Every assertion is backed by lived experience. He does not teach theory. He teaches what he has done, what failed, and what worked — with the receipts to prove it.

"I have sat in every seat: rep, manager, owner. Here is what your team is thinking."
"Research suggests that effective leadership..."
3. Reframe the problem

The audience thinks they have a hiring problem. He shows them they have a management problem. They think they need more leads. He shows them they need fewer leaks. This is how belief shifts happen.

"Your reps are not inconsistent because they are lazy. They are inconsistent because the system is broken."
"There are multiple factors that contribute to rep underperformance."
4. Human enough to trust

The vulnerability is deliberate and load-bearing. Without the origin story, the authority has no root. The voice must carry both the edge and the empathy. Neither alone is enough.

"I was the rep that got failed by management. That is why I can tell you what your team is thinking."
"As someone with extensive industry experience..."

The brand challenges; it does not lecture. There is a meaningful difference between the two.

Not Arrogant

Not Vague

Not Desperate

The offer is made once, clearly, and confidently. It is not repeated or hedged into apology.

10 — Logomark

The Mark

KW
What it means

The ram is Aries: the first sign. Associated with primal drive, forward momentum, and the willingness to charge when others hold back. The sword represents precision and decisiveness — not aggression for its own sake, but the ability to cut through noise and act with clarity.

Together: a fighter who is also disciplined. A force that has direction.

Usage rules
  • On dark backgrounds: Cream/off-white (#F5EFE0)
  • On light backgrounds: Dark charcoal (#1E1E1C)
  • Never recolour the mark in brand accent orange
  • Never apply shadows, gradients, or effects
  • Always maintain clear space equal to the ram's body height
  • KW monogram may be used independently as a watermark
11 — Colour Palette

The Warmth

Dark Slate #2F2F2D Secondary BG
Burnt Orange #C85A20 Primary Accent
Warm Amber #E8843A Secondary Accent
Warm Cream #F5EFE0 Primary Text
Dusty Blush #EAD5C0 Secondary Text
Palette logic

The entire palette is warm-toned. There are no cool greys, no blues, and no clinical whites. The warmth signals organic authority, earned experience, and human grit. It is the opposite of corporate polish.

Burnt orange is used sparingly. Its power comes entirely from restraint. Charcoal dominates. Warm Cream on Charcoal is the brand's signature combination.

Do not use
  • Pure white (#FFFFFF) — use Warm Cream instead
  • Cool greys or blues — incompatible with brand warmth
  • Bright, saturated colours — break the cinematic register
  • Neon or high-contrast accent colours of any kind
12 — Typography

The Tension

The brand uses a two-font system built around deliberate contrast: condensed heavy grotesque for structural force, and elegant cursive italic for personal humanity. The contrast between the two is the brand identity made visible.

Headline font — Condensed Heavy Grotesque (800–900 weight, all caps)
Ascension of Value
Extremely heavy weight. All caps. Tight tracking. Used for slide titles, statement headlines, and confrontational assertions. The weight communicates directness and force before the words are even read.
Script accent — Elegant Cursive Italic (1–2 words only, in Burnt Orange or Warm Cream)
I Walked Away
Used for the emotional or human word in a headline pairing. Maximum three words. Never a full sentence. Never in body copy. Rendered in Burnt Orange or Warm Cream only.

13 — Imagery Guidelines

The Visual World

Grit meets aspiration. The feeling of a long road being walked, not a finish line being celebrated.

Colour treatment
Treatment 01

Black and white / desaturated. High contrast, gritty, timeless. Used for the most intense and personal moments.

Treatment 02

Warm amber and orange toned. The brand's signature. Sunset-lit, fire-adjacent, aspirational. Used for conceptual moments.

Treatment 03

Muted natural colour. Slightly desaturated real-world photography. Used for action shots and community moments.

  • People in motion: running, training, moving forward. Often motion-blurred.
  • Figures from behind or in silhouette, so the audience can project themselves in.
  • Vast natural landscapes: mountains, open plains, desert, night sky, dense forest.
  • Raw environments: corridors, athletic tracks, outdoor terrain.
  • Training environments: gyms, fight camps, early mornings.
  • Quiet personal moments: reflection, solitude, the pause before action.
  • Polished corporate stock photography.
  • Staged smiling headshots.
  • Bright office environments.
  • Abstract 3D tech visuals.
  • Generic motivational imagery, including sunsets with quote text overlaid.
14 — Slide and Content Design Principles

Six Patterns

Pattern 01

Full-bleed photography with overlaid type

The image fills the entire frame. Text sits directly over the photograph, treated as a design element rather than a caption. Type is large and confident. The most common and most powerful format.

Pattern 02

The inverted pyramid

Visual shorthand for hierarchical frameworks. Dark charcoal layered triangles with warm amber and orange gradient photography bleeding in from the base. Used for the Rep Lifecycle and Ascension of Value.

Pattern 03

Dark background with structured text

For detailed, instructional content. Category heading in heavy condensed caps. Body text below in light weight. Right-aligned text blocks with generous whitespace. Clean, architectural, authoritative.

Pattern 04

Single-concept reveal

One powerful statement. Maximum negative space. The pause-and-land moment. The most emotionally impactful format when used with restraint. Never overuse it.

Pattern 05

Light background contrast slide

Warm Cream or Dusty Blush backgrounds used for structural or conceptual transitions. Creates breathing room between intense dark sequences without breaking the brand world.

Pattern 06

The orange bracket device

A single vertical Burnt Orange line or bracket used to group and emphasise a stack of text items. Minimal. Architectural. Precise. A signature micro-detail in commitment statements and key principles.
