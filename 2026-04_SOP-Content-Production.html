01 — Purpose

What This Document Is

This SOP governs how content is produced for Kacper Wiloch across Instagram, LinkedIn, and Threads. It is the operating layer beneath the brand guidelines and the fortnightly content plan. It names the people, the sequence, the deadlines, and the decision points.

Every piece of content for Kacper runs on a two-week production cycle. The cadence, pillars, and content types are already locked in the fortnightly content plan. This document is about how the work gets made, by whom, and when.

Brief on Friday. Shoot on Saturday. Edit and review early week. Post on schedule. The cadence protects the quality.

02 — Team and Responsibilities

Five roles. Clear ownership at every stage. Nothing should ever be "in the middle" without a named person accountable for moving it forward.

Brand QA · Value Prop Alignment

Owns brand QA across all output and ensures messaging tracks with Kacper’s core value proposition and broader corporate strategy. Co-runs QA with Juan and holds the client-facing relationship end-to-end.

  • Writes carousel copy and provides a design brief to Lucy
  • Manages publishing on Instagram (reels, carousels, grid)

Owns all written copy and publishing on LinkedIn and Threads. Works with more latitude than on reels or carousels — the guardrails are the brand voice guide and the format library, not a per-post brief.

03 — Production Lanes

Three Lanes, Three Workflows

Reels, carousels, and written posts each move through a different production chain. The person who owns each step is named. Nothing is ambiguous.

Lane 01 Reels · Talking Head + B-Roll Juan → Kacper → Noah → Juan & Jack
01
Juan · Brief
Write Talking Points and Shot Brief
One document per reel. Names the format code (IG·R1, R2, R3, etc.), the hook, 3–5 talking points, the CTA, and any B-roll references. Delivered to Notion by Friday of the prior week so Kacper has it ahead of any weekend or earlier-in-the-week shoot.
02
Juan & Jack · Brief Review
Align on Strategy and Brand Before Shoot
Juan and Jack review all reel briefs as a bundle by Thursday of the prior week. Juan owns strategic direction; Jack checks brand and value-prop alignment. Anything needing rework is handled before Kacper shoots.
03
Kacper · Shoot
Film Batched Talking Heads (Saturday Minimum)
All five reel talking heads for the week filmed from Juan’s approved briefs. Saturday is the default batch day; Kacper may elect to shoot earlier in the week. S-Log3 standard. Raw footage uploaded to the fortnight’s Drive folder and flagged in Slack as soon as it lands.
04
Noah · Edit
Full Week Edit Window — Mon to Fri
Kacper hands raw footage to Noah on Monday morning. Noah works through the edits across the full week: grade, typography, overlays, CTA cards. All five reels delivered to Notion by Friday, ready to publish the following week. The one-week window protects edit quality at volume.
05
Juan & Jack · Joint QA
Strategy and Brand Review
Juan reviews for strategy and format fidelity (does the hook land? does the CTA match? does the edit serve the brief?). Jack reviews for brand and value-prop alignment. Feedback consolidated into one revision pass back to Noah. Target turnaround: same day.
06
Kacper · Final Approval
Authenticity Check and Greenlight
Kacper reviews each approved cut. 24-hour turnaround. Once greenlit, Juan publishes to Instagram on the scheduled date.
Lane 02 Carousels · Educational + Lifestyle Juan → Lucy → Juan & Jack
01
Juan · Copy + Brief
Write Slide Copy and Design Brief
One document per carousel. Full copy slide-by-slide. Named format (IG·C1, C2, C3). Clear message, target audience state, design notes (tone, reference patterns from the brand guide). Delivered to Notion by Thursday of the prior week.
02
Juan & Jack · Copy Review
Align Before Design Begins
Juan and Jack review the copy together before Lucy starts. If the hook or message needs sharpening, it happens here — not at the design stage. Thursday content meeting is the natural check-in for this.
03
Lucy · Design
Produce Carousel to Brand Spec
Lucy designs from the approved brief and copy. Applies brand system: condensed heavy headline with cursive script accent, warm cream on charcoal, photography treatments per brand guide. Full deck delivered to Notion at least 3 working days before publish date.
04
Juan & Jack · Joint QA
Strategy and Brand Review
Juan reviews for hook quality and format fidelity. Jack reviews for brand system integrity and value-prop alignment. Consolidated feedback to Lucy. One revision round. Target turnaround: 1 working day.
05
Kacper · Final Approval
Approve and Move to Publish
Kacper reviews and approves the final deck within 24 hours. Juan publishes to Instagram on the scheduled date.
Lane 03 LinkedIn & Threads · Written Tommy → Juan & Jack → Kacper → Tommy publishes
01
Tommy · Draft
Write Against the Format Library
Tommy works from the 7-format LinkedIn rotation (LI·Mon through LI·Sun) and the Threads format library (Echo, Question, List, Receipt, Observation, Series). Full week of LinkedIn + full Threads batch delivered to Notion by Friday of the prior week.
02
Juan & Jack · Joint QA
Strategy and Brand Review
Juan reviews for hook quality, format compliance, and strategic alignment across the week’s arc. Jack reviews for voice, brand, and value-prop alignment. Edits in Notion or flag for Tommy to revise. Target turnaround: 1 working day.
03
Kacper · Final Read
Approve Voice
Kacper reads final drafts for voice authenticity and factual accuracy on any claims, numbers, or case studies. Approves in Notion.
04
Tommy · Publish
Post to LinkedIn and Threads
Tommy publishes LinkedIn posts on the scheduled day. Threads batch (7 posts) scheduled Monday morning via a scheduler or posted live through the week per agreed rhythm. All publish confirmations logged in Notion.

05 — Notion: Source of Truth

Every piece of content — brief, copy, design, edit, approval status — lives in the Merigrove Content Notion workspace. Nothing shipped to production lives only in Slack or email. If it isn’t in Notion, it doesn’t exist.

06 — QA Standards

What Every Piece Must Pass

Three lenses across two QA passes. Juan and Jack run joint QA together. Kacper does the final authenticity read. Each lens asks different questions.

Juan · Strategy & Format QA

  • Does it match the strategic intent of the brief?
  • Is the hook doing the work it needs to do?
  • Is the target belief shift or insight landing clearly?
  • Does the pace and structure respect the named format?
  • Is the piece fit for the analytics signal we’re testing?

Jack · Brand & Value Prop QA

  • Does the voice match the brand guide? Direct, diagnostic, earned.
  • Does the messaging track with the core value proposition?
  • British English throughout. No em-dashes.
  • Visual system intact: palette, typography pairing, photography treatment.
  • No claims that aren’t backed by Kacper’s lived experience.

Kacper · Authenticity QA

  • Does this sound like something he would actually say?
  • Are the numbers, claims, and case studies accurate?
  • Is any personal context (family, fight training, clients) handled respectfully?
  • Is the CTA one he is willing to stand behind?
  • Final authority on publish, always.

Hard Stops · Auto-Reject Triggers

  • Em-dashes anywhere in the copy
  • US spelling or phrasing
  • Vocabulary from the “avoid” list (hustle, grind, hack, mindset reset, etc.)
  • Generic stock or motivational imagery
  • Claims without proof when proof is required
07 — Cadence Rituals

The Meetings That Run the Machine

Four recurring rituals keep the production chain honest. None of them are optional.

Weekly
Monday · 30 min
Monday Content Meeting

Who: Full content team (Juan, Tommy, Lucy, Noah, Jack). Purpose: Kacper hands raw footage from the weekend shoot to Noah. Confirm everything uploaded cleanly. Noah opens the week-long edit window. Any production blockers surfaced and assigned. Publishing slate for the week confirmed.

Weekly
Thursday · 30 min
Thursday Content Meeting

Who: Full content team. Purpose: Confirm the following week’s briefs are ready for Friday handover to Kacper. Final tweaks on reel briefs, carousel copy, and written drafts. Juan and Jack joint-QA outstanding pieces live where useful. Lucy flags any carousel status risks.

Fortnightly
Monday · 60 min
Strategy Kick-off · Juan-led

Who: Juan, Jack, Kacper. Purpose: Juan presents the previous fortnight’s analytics and proposes the strategic direction for the next cycle. Confirm thematics, hooks, and tactical priorities. Lock the slot-level content plan. Greenlight the experiment block for Week 2 Wednesday.

Fortnightly
End of Cycle · 45 min
Retrospective & Analytics Review · Juan-led

Who: Juan, Jack. Purpose: Juan pulls performance data by format code, pillar, and platform. Which IG·R types outperformed? Which LI days drove the most engagement? Which Threads formats carried? Juan ties insights directly to the next fortnight’s strategy. This is where meaningful analytics enter the system.

08 — Escalation and Exceptions

When Things Go Sideways

The production chain is built to absorb small friction without breaking. When bigger issues surface, there is a clear path forward — surfaced in the Thursday content meeting where possible, or directly between Juan and Jack where timing demands it.

Brief misses Friday handoverThe piece rolls forward to the next slot of the same format in the calendar. Juan and Jack decide together whether to backfill from the evergreen reserve or leave the slot dark.
Kacper shoot slipsIf neither a Saturday nor an earlier-week slot lands before brief handover, draw from the evergreen reserve. Juan adjusts the publishing schedule for the affected week. No panic shoots — cadence matters more than any single piece.
Edit misses Friday deliveryNoah flags in the Thursday content meeting as soon as it’s at risk. Juan and Jack decide whether to delay the affected reel by a week or swap the slot with an evergreen reserve piece.
Carousel misses 3-day windowLucy flags in the Thursday content meeting or earlier via Slack. Juan and Jack decide together whether to delay publish or accept a truncated revision window.
Major voice or brand departureAny team member can flag a piece that feels significantly off-brand or off-voice. Juan and Jack review together and decide to hold, revise, or publish. Ownership of the flag is not ownership of the decision.
Experiment block idea doesn’t landDefault to a standard IG·R3 diagnostic reel for Week 2 Wednesday. The slot is never left empty, but the experiment returns next fortnight.
Performance anomalyA single piece underperforming by 50%+ against baseline is logged in Notion for Juan to factor into the retrospective. Not a crisis — data for the fortnightly review.

The evergreen reserve: three pre-approved talking-head reels on Kacper’s strongest authority topics, filmed once a quarter, held for emergency use. No other unapproved content goes live under any circumstance.
